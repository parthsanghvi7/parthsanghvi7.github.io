Plotting using vega-lite

Bar Chart #1

  • I used a bar chart to visualize the trend of mean square footage distribution over buildings grouped by Agency Name. Since the data on X-axis is nominal, using a bar chart is the best way to move forward. Bar chart will properly quantify the Square Footage values of each of the agency names. For the Y-axis, we will use ‘mean’ as aggregate function, hence mean of square footage value is calculated for each agency. We use ‘orange’ as color value for our visual. I am trying to highlight the average square footage occupied by each agency. We can see that buildings of department of revenue cover the largest amount of square footage. It is an outlier among other categories.

Bar Chart #2

  • Next, I used a bar chart to visualize the trend of mean square footage distribution over grouped building status type. Since the data on X-axis is nominal, using a bar chart is the best way to move forward. Bar chart will properly quantify the Square Footage values of each of the Building Status category. For the Y-axis, we will use ‘mean’ as aggregate function, hence mean of square footage value is calculated for each Building Status Category. We use ‘cyan’ as color value for our visual. I am trying to highlight how much area on average is used by buildings of each building status category. From the visual we can see that most of the in progress buildings have the highest square footage.

Line Plot

  • Next, I used a lined chart to visualize the trend of median square footage over Year constructed. Since square footage can be shown using numbers, the type will be quantitative. A line chart will give us information on when were the buildings constructed majorly. Further, to enhance my visualization I have used the ‘blue‘ color. I am trying to see the trend of building construction over time.From the visual it is evident that many of the buildings were constructed in the mid-1800 to mid-1900.
